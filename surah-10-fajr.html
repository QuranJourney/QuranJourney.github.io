Surah Al-Fajr

The Dawn • Makkah • 30 Verses
The Dawn of the Soul. A journey through the ruins of ancient tyrants, demonstrating the futility of worldly power. It breaks down the ego's false metric of defining God's love by wealth, ending with the ultimate spiritual destination: The Tranquil Soul.
Verse 1
وَٱلْفَجْرِ
"By the dawn"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the time of dawn, when the light breaks the darkness.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The dawn signifies the daily rebirth of the world. Just as the night inevitably breaks into morning, the darkness of tyranny in Makkah will inevitably be broken by the light of Islam.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Fajr is the time of the greatest spiritual opening. It is the victory of the soul over the heavy, dark sleep of the ego. Awakening at this time prepares the heart for illumination.
Knowledge & SignsSpiritual Practice
Verse 2
وَلَيَالٍ عَشْرٍۢ
"And [by] ten nights"
Plain Understanding
An oath by ten specific, highly significant nights.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The overwhelming majority of scholars agree these are the first ten days of Dhul-Hijjah (the days of Hajj), which the Prophet ﷺ said are the best days of the entire year for righteous deeds.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Allah honors specific times and places to give the forgetful soul "seasons of spiritual profit." He multiplies the reward during these times out of pure Generosity.
Spiritual PracticeAttributes of Allah
Verse 3
وَٱلشَّفْعِ وَٱلْوَتْرِ
"And [by] the even and the odd"
Plain Understanding
An oath by everything that is paired (even) and everything that is single (odd).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"The Odd" refers to Allah, the One, the Unique who has no partner. "The Even" refers to creation, because all created things exist in pairs (male/female, light/dark, life/death).
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The spiritual path is recognizing the Odd (The Creator) behind the Even (the creation). Do not let the multiplicity of the world blind you to the Singularity of its Maker.
Attributes of AllahKnowledge & Signs
Verse 4
وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ
"And [by] the night when it passes,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the night as it journeys on and fades away.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not despair over a state of darkness, for it is constantly moving toward the light." The night is temporary. Hardship, oppression, and spiritual dryness are states that are actively passing.

Verse 5
هَلْ فِى ذَٰلِكَ قَسَمٌۭ لِّذِى حِجْرٍ
"Is there [not] in [all] that an oath [sufficient] for one of perception?"
Plain Understanding
Are these oaths not enough evidence for anyone with a sound intellect?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Hijr" means intellect or reason. It literally means "a barrier," because a sound intellect acts as a barrier, preventing a person from doing foolish or destructive things.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
True intelligence is not academic prowess; it is the ability to perceive the signs of God in the passing of time and the structure of reality. A genius who denies God has no "Hijr."

Verse 6
أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِعَادٍ
"Have you not considered how your Lord dealt with 'Aad -"
Plain Understanding
Look at history: Do you not see how God destroyed the powerful ancient tribe of 'Aad?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The tribe of the Prophet Hud. They were giants, possessing immense physical strength and wealth, living in the southern Arabian peninsula. They thought their power made them untouchable.

Verse 7
إِرَمَ ذَاتِ ٱلْعِمَادِ
"[With] Iram - who had lofty pillars,"
Plain Understanding
The people of Iram, known for their massive, towering architecture.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
They built high pillars to reach the sky, symbolizing the towering arrogance of their egos. But when you build your foundation on pride rather than piety, a single wind of Divine decree will topple it.

Verse 8
ٱلَّتِى لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُهَا فِى ٱلْبِلَٰدِ
"The likes of whom had never been created in the land?"
Plain Understanding
They built a civilization and a city so magnificent that nothing like it had ever existed on earth.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not let the beauty of what He has given them deceive you." Their unique civilization did not give them a unique exception from the laws of God. Grandeur cannot save you from justice.
Human NatureDivine Justice
Verse 9
وَثَمُودَ ٱلَّذِينَ جَابُوا۟ ٱلصَّخْرَ بِٱلْوَادِ
"And [with] Thamud, who carved out the rocks in the valley?"
Plain Understanding
And look at the tribe of Thamud, who had the engineering mastery to carve entire cities into solid mountain rock.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The tribe of the Prophet Salih (at Mada'in Salih). They carved into mountains to create indestructible homes to protect themselves from death and disaster. God destroyed them with a single sound wave (a shriek from heaven).
Prophetic HistoryDivine Justice
Verse 10
وَفِرْعَوْنَ ذِى ٱلْأَوْتَادِ
"And [with] Pharaoh, owner of the stakes?"
Plain Understanding
And look at Pharaoh, the master of stakes/pyramids, who ruled with an iron fist.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Awtad" (stakes) refers to his massive armies, his pyramids, or his method of torture (nailing opponents to the ground with stakes). He claimed to be God himself.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "Pharaoh" is the ultimate archetype of the Nafs (Ego). It demands worship, claims ownership of the rivers (resources), and uses stakes (control) to maintain dominance.
Prophetic HistoryArrogance
Verse 11
ٱلَّذِينَ طَغَوْا۟ فِى ٱلْبِلَٰدِ
"[All of] whom oppressed within the lands,"
Plain Understanding
All three of these civilizations ('Aad, Thamud, Pharaoh) became tyrants and crossed all limits in their respective regions.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Tughyan" (transgression) is the natural result of feeling self-sufficient. When the ego feels no need for God, it becomes a tyrant over creation.
Human NatureArrogance
Verse 12
فَأَكْثَرُوا۟ فِيهَا ٱلْفَسَادَ
"And increased therein the corruption."
Plain Understanding
And they spread massive corruption, injustice, and rot throughout the earth.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Outer corruption is always a manifestation of inner corruption. They corrupted the land because their hearts were corrupt with greed and pride.

Verse 13
فَصَبَّ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّكَ سَوْطَ عَذَابٍ
"So your Lord poured upon them a scourge of punishment."
Plain Understanding
Because of their tyranny, God whipped them with a relentless downpour of destruction.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Sawt" literally means a whip or a lash. Allah uses this to show that despite their massive pillars and carved mountains, their destruction was as easy for God as striking a disobedient slave with a whip. It was a swift, severe pouring of wrath.
PunishmentDivine Justice
Verse 14
إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِٱلْمِرْصَادِ
"Indeed, your Lord is in observation."
Plain Understanding
Your Lord is in an ambush-point, constantly watching and monitoring everything they do.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Mirsad" is a place of ambush. The Quraish thought they were getting away with torturing the Muslims. Allah warns them: I am watching, and the ambush is already set.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The best of your moments is the one in which you witness your poverty and realize your lowliness." Knowing God is in the "watchtower" cures the delusion of hidden sins.
Attributes of AllahThe UnseenAccountability
Verse 15
فَأَمَّا ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ إِذَا مَا ٱبْتَلَىٰهُ رَبُّهُۥ فَأَكْرَمَهُۥ وَنَعَّمَهُۥ فَيَقُولُ رَبِّىٓ أَكْرَمَنِ
"And as for man, when his Lord tries him and [thus] is generous to him and favors him, he says, 'My Lord has honored me.'"
Plain Understanding
When God tests a human by giving them wealth and a comfortable life, the human arrogantly assumes: "God loves me; I deserve this honor."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Allah calls wealth a "test" (Ibtila), but the blind human calls it an "honor" (Ikram). This is a fatal misdiagnosis. Wealth is a tool given to see if you will be grateful and generous, not a trophy for your ego.

Verse 16
وَأَمَّآ إِذَا مَا ٱبْتَلَىٰهُ فَقَدَرَ عَلَيْهِ رِزْقَهُۥ فَيَقُولُ رَبِّىٓ أَهَانَنِ
"But when He tries him and restricts his provision, he says, 'My Lord has humiliated me.'"
Plain Understanding
Conversely, when God tests a human by restricting their income and making life hard, the human complains: "God hates me and has humiliated me."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Sometimes He gives to you but deprives you (spiritually); and sometimes He deprives you but gives to you (spiritually)." The ego measures God's love by the size of its bank account. The Sufi knows that sometimes poverty is the greatest honor, as it strips away distractions and forces reliance on God.
Human NatureTestPatience
Verse 17
كَلَّا ۖ بَل لَّا تُكْرِمُونَ ٱلْيَتِيمَ
"No! But you do not honor the orphan,"
Plain Understanding
Absolutely wrong! Your logic is flawed. Poverty isn't humiliation. You know what real humiliation is? It is your refusal to take care of the orphans in your society.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The pre-Islamic Arabs were obsessed with tribal power. Orphans had no power, so they were ignored, abused, and their inheritances were stolen by their uncles. Allah redefines honor: honor is not how much gold you have, but how you treat the weakest among you.

Verse 18
وَلَا تَحَـٰٓضُّونَ عَلَىٰ طَعَامِ ٱلْمِسْكِينِ
"And you do not encourage one another to feed the poor."
Plain Understanding
And you don't even bother to urge your friends and family to feed the starving.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Notice the wording: not only do you not feed them, you don't even *encourage* others to do it. The heart has become so hard that the very concept of collective compassion is dead.

Verse 19
وَتَأْكُلُونَ ٱلتُّرَاثَ أَكْلًا لَّمًّا
"And you consume inheritance, devouring [it] altogether,"
Plain Understanding
You greedily swallow up the inheritances of others, mixing your lawful money with unlawful stolen money without a second thought.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
In Jahiliyyah (pre-Islamic ignorance), women and children were not allowed to inherit. The strongest male relative would sweep up the entire estate for himself.

Verse 20
وَتُحِبُّونَ ٱلْمَالَ حُبًّا جَمًّا
"And you love wealth with immense love."
Plain Understanding
And your root disease is an overwhelming, obsessive love for hoarding money.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The heart is the temple of the Lord." When you love wealth with "immense love," you place an idol in the temple. Wealth should reside in the hand to be distributed, not in the heart to be loved.

Verse 21
كَلَّآ إِذَا دُكَّتِ ٱلْأَرْضُ دَكًّا دَكًّا
"No! When the earth has been leveled - pounded and crushed -"
Plain Understanding
Stop this behavior! Wake up! The reality hits when this entire earth is pulverized into flat, flat dust.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The repetition ("Dakkan dakka") emphasizes the continuous, violent shattering of everything humans built. The mansions bought with stolen inheritance will become powder.

Verse 22
وَجَآءَ رَبُّكَ وَٱلْمَلَكُ صَفًّا صَفًّا
"And your Lord has come and the angels, rank upon rank,"
Plain Understanding
And the Majesty of your Lord arrives to judge, with the angels descending in massive, organized, terrifying rows.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ultimate manifestation of Divine Jalal (Majesty). The ego, which thought it was a king on earth, shrinks to absolute zero before the organized descent of the heavenly armies.
Attributes of AllahThe Unseen
Verse 23
وَجِا۟ىٓءَ يَوْمَئِذٍۭ بِجَهَنَّمَ ۚ يَوْمَئِذٍ يَتَذَكَّرُ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ وَأَنَّىٰ لَهُ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ
"And brought [within view], that Day, is Hell - that Day, man will remember, but what good to him will be the remembrance?"
Plain Understanding
Hellfire is literally dragged into view. On that exact day, the human being will suddenly wake up and "get it"—but his realization is entirely too late to save him.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ said that Hell will be brought forth on that Day, pulled by 70,000 ropes, with 70,000 angels pulling each rope. When humanity sees it, the veil of the Dunya drops permanently.

Verse 24
يَقُولُ يَـٰلَيْتَنِى قَدَّمْتُ لِحَيَاتِى
"He will say, 'Oh, I wish I had sent ahead [some good] for my life.'"
Plain Understanding
Filled with regret, he will cry out: "If only I had invested in my REAL life!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Notice he says "for MY life." He finally realizes that his 70 years on earth were not "life"; they were just the waiting room. True life (Hayawan) is the eternal realm. He regrets hoarding wealth instead of sending it ahead to his eternal estate.
AccountabilityHopeHuman Nature
Verse 25
فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَّا يُعَذِّبُ عَذَابَهُۥٓ أَحَدٌ
"So on that Day, none will punish [as severely as] His punishment,"
Plain Understanding
On that Judgment Day, no one in the universe can punish the way God punishes the tyrant.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Pharaoh and the chiefs of Makkah tortured believers brutally. Allah responds: You think you know how to inflict pain? The punishment of the Creator of the nervous system is incomparable to the puny torture of men.
Divine JusticePunishment
Verse 26
وَلَا يُوثِقُ وَثَاقَهُۥٓ أَحَدٌ
"And none will bind [as severely as] His binding [of the evildoers]."
Plain Understanding
And no one can tie up and shackle an enemy the way God will bind the guilty.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
If you bound yourself to your desires in the Dunya, you will be bound by chains in the Akhirah. True freedom is submitting to the Divine Law now, avoiding the eternal chains later.
Divine JusticePunishment
Verse 27
يَـٰٓأَيَّتُهَا ٱلنَّفْسُ ٱلْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ
"O tranquil soul,"
Plain Understanding
Amidst all this terror, God turns to the believer and says: "O soul that is at absolute peace."
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
This is the summit of Sufism. The Nafs (soul) goes through stages: The commanding soul (Ammarah), the blaming soul (Lawwamah), and finally the tranquil soul (Mutma'innah). It is tranquil because it relies entirely on God. Neither wealth nor poverty, nor the terror of Judgment Day, can shake its deep peace.
Spiritual PracticeJannah
Verse 28
ٱرْجِعِىٓ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةً مَّرْضِيَّةً
"Return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing [to Him],"
Plain Understanding
Come back to your Creator. You are deeply satisfied with Him, and He is completely satisfied with you.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"If you want to know your standing with Him, look at where He has placed you." You were content (Rida) with His decrees in the world—even the painful ones—so He rewards you with absolute contentment in the Next. Mutual pleasure is the highest paradise.
Attributes of AllahHopeJannah
Verse 29
فَٱدْخُلِى فِى عِبَـٰدِى
"And enter among My [righteous] servants"
Plain Understanding
Join the ranks of My chosen, elite, and beloved worshippers.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The highest honor is to be called an "'Abd" (servant) of God. By telling the soul to enter among "My servants," He is welcoming them into the most exclusive, noble company in existence.
Spiritual PracticeHumility
Verse 30
وَٱدْخُلِى جَنَّتِى
"And enter My Paradise."
Plain Understanding
And walk into the garden I have personally prepared for you.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Notice the possessive pronoun "My" (Jannati). It is not just a garden of rivers and trees; it is the garden of Proximity. He does not say "enter Paradise," He says "enter MY Paradise." The reward is the closeness to the Host.
