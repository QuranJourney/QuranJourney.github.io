Surah Ash-Sharh

The Relief • Makkah • 8 Verses
The Anatomy of Inner Peace. A deeply intimate Surah detailing the spiritual surgery God performed on the Prophet's ﷺ heart. It teaches us that true relief from crushing anxiety is found not in changing the world around us, but in the expansion of the chest within us.
Verse 1
أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ
"Did We not expand for you, [O Muhammad], your breast?"
Plain Understanding
A rhetorical, comforting question: Did We not open and widen your heart to receive guidance, courage, and peace?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This refers to the physical event where the Angel Jibreel opened the Prophet's chest and washed his heart with Zamzam water, removing the "portion of Satan." It also refers to the spiritual expansion (Sharh) required to bear the massive weight of the Quran and the psychological abuse of the Quraish.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "expansion of the chest" is the ultimate spiritual cure. When the ego is in control, the chest feels tight, restricted, and reactive to every insult. When Allah expands the chest with light (Nur), the anxieties of the Dunya become incredibly small. A constricted chest reacts with anger; an expanded chest reacts with mercy.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Expansion (Bast) is a gift of His grace. Do not let the expansion make you forget your servitude, just as you should not let contraction (Qabd) make you despair." Recognize who caused the expansion.

Verse 2
وَوَضَعْنَا عَنكَ وِزْرَكَ
"And We removed from you your burden"
Plain Understanding
And did We not lift off the heavy weight that was dragging you down?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Before revelation, the Prophet ﷺ carried the immense grief of seeing his society drowning in idolatry and corruption without knowing how to fix it. The revelation of Islam lifted this psychological and spiritual burden.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The heaviest burden a human carries is the illusion of control—the ego. Allah removed this burden from the Prophet ﷺ by teaching him absolute reliance (Tawakkul). When you stop trying to carry the world and let the Creator carry it, the burden vanishes.

Verse 3
ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنقَضَ ظَهْرَكَ
"Which had weighed upon your back"
Plain Understanding
The burden that was so severe it felt like it was breaking your back.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He makes you feel the crushing weight of your own weakness so that you can intimately experience the relief of His strength." You must feel the back-breaking nature of the burden to truly taste the sweetness of the relief.

Verse 4
وَرَفَعْنَا لَكَ ذِكْرَكَ
"And raised high for you your repute."
Plain Understanding
And We elevated your name and your remembrance high above all others.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Quraish spent all their resources trying to smear and erase his name. Allah's response was to attach the Prophet's name to His own Name in the Shahadah (La ilaha illallah, Muhammadur rasulullah). Every time the Adhan is called around the earth, his name is raised high.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
True elevation (Rif'ah) comes solely from the Divine, not from PR campaigns. When a servant utterly humbles himself before Allah, Allah forces the heavens and the earth to honor that servant's name.

Verse 5
فَإِنَّ مَعَ ٱلْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا
"For indeed, with hardship [will be] ease."
Plain Understanding
So truly, running alongside every difficulty is a relief.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A profound linguistic rule in Arabic: "Al-Usr" (Hardship) has the definite article "Al" (The), making it one specific hardship. "Yusra" (Ease) is indefinite, meaning vast, multiple, or unbounded ease. Thus, one hardship cannot overpower multiple eases.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not despair of a hardship... for the ease is born from the very womb of the hardship." Notice Allah says *with* (Ma'a) hardship, not *after* (Ba'da). God's grace and hidden ease are present inside the trial itself, actively working to deliver you.

Verse 6
إِنَّ مَعَ ٱلْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا
"Indeed, with hardship [will be] ease."
Plain Understanding
Reiteration for absolute certainty: Truly, right alongside the difficulty is the ease.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Why repeat it? Because the anxious ego is loud and forgets quickly. Repetition anchors trust (Tawakkul) in the heart. The first statement breaks the despair; the second statement cements the hope.

Verse 7
فَإِذَا فَرَغْتَ فَٱنصَبْ
"So when you have finished [your duties], then stand up [for worship]."
Plain Understanding
Therefore, when you are done with your worldly tasks and preaching, stand up and exert yourself in prayer.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Allah tells the Prophet ﷺ that the expansion of the chest and the promise of ease are not excuses to relax. Rather, relief from worldly anxiety should free your energy to plunge deeply into intense spiritual worship.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The believer has no concept of "killing time" or pure idleness. Rest from the exhaustion of the Dunya is found not in sleep, but in the life-giving exertion of worship. Worship is the vacation of the soul.
CommandsSpiritual Practice
Verse 8
وَإِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ فَٱرْغَب
"And to your Lord direct [your] longing."
Plain Understanding
And make your Creator the sole, exclusive focus of all your desires, hopes, and longing.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Let not the intention of your heart be anything other than Him. That which is beside Him cannot sustain your hopes." True longing (Raghba) means emptying the heart of the creation so that only the Creator remains. If you long for Him alone, you will never be disappointed.
