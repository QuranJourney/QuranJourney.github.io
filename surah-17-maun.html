Mushaf: #107 | Chronological: #17

Surah Al-Ma'un

The Small Kindnesses • Makkah • 7 Verses
The Unmasking of Hypocrisy. A chilling reminder that religious rituals without social compassion are entirely worthless. It exposes how the denial of the Afterlife directly produces cruelty toward the vulnerable, and how showing off in prayer is a curse rather than a cure.
Verse 1
أَرَءَيْتَ ٱلَّذِى يُكَذِّبُ بِٱلدِّينِ
"Have you seen the one who denies the Recompense?"
Plain Understanding
Have you looked closely at the type of person who completely rejects the concept of the Day of Judgment?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Addressed to the Prophet ﷺ, referring to specific leaders in Makkah (like Abu Sufyan before his conversion, or Abu Jahl) who used to slaughter massive camels for feasts to show off their wealth, but would brutally beat away any poor person who asked for a scrap.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
True belief in the Hereafter (Ad-Din) is not just an intellectual statement; it is a state of the heart that radically alters your behavior. If a person is cruel, it is absolute proof that their heart practically denies the Judgment, regardless of what their tongue claims.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The root of every disobedience, heedlessness, and passion is satisfaction with the self." The one who denies the recompense is entirely satisfied with the Dunya, seeing no need to invest in the Unseen.

Verse 2
فَذَٰلِكَ ٱلَّذِى يَدُعُّ ٱلْيَتِيمَ
"For that is the one who drives away the orphan"
Plain Understanding
That denier is the exact same person who violently and harshly shoves away the orphan.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Yadu'" means to push violently with aggression. It refers to Abu Jahl, who literally pushed and struck an orphan who had come to him asking for his own father's inheritance, which Abu Jahl was hoarding.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The ego worships power. Because the orphan has no worldly power, no tribe to defend him, and no money to offer in return, the ego feels no need to respect him. How you treat those who can do nothing for you is the ultimate mirror of your soul.

Verse 3
وَلَا يَحُضُّ عَلَىٰ طَعَامِ ٱلْمِسْكِينِ
"And does not encourage the feeding of the poor."
Plain Understanding
And not only does he refuse to feed the starving person himself, he doesn't even bother to urge others to do it.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A "Miskin" is someone whose poverty is so severe that it has literally immobilized them (from the root 'Sukun', meaning stillness). The tyrant's heart is so dead that he feels absolutely zero empathy for the immobilized, starving human being.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
A heart devoid of mercy for the creation is entirely devoid of connection to the Creator. You cannot claim to love the Maker while violently despising His masterpieces.

Verse 4
فَوَيْلٌۭ لِّلْمُصَلِّينَ
"So woe to those who pray"
Plain Understanding
Therefore, destruction and a curse be upon those who perform the prayer!
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A shocking, terrifying verse. "Wayl" means severe destruction, or a specific, horrific valley in Hellfire. Why would God curse people who are praying? The answer is immediately provided in the next verses. It is the ultimate warning against hollow rituals.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Prayer without presence is a physical exercise, not a spiritual ascension. If your body is in Sujud (prostration) but your heart is standing in arrogance, your prayer becomes a witness against you, not a savior for you.

Verse 5
ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ عَن صَلَاتِهِمْ سَاهُونَ
"[But] who are heedless of their prayer -"
Plain Understanding
Woe to those who pray, but are completely neglectful, absent-minded, and lazy regarding their prayers.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Sahun" (heedless) refers to hypocrites who delay the prayer until its time is almost gone, rush through the physical movements without knowing what they are saying, and feel absolutely no remorse if they completely miss it.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"To stand before the King while your heart is conversing with the marketplace is the height of bad manners (Su' al-Adab)." Heedlessness in prayer is a symptom of a heart that values the Dunya infinitely more than the Divine meeting.
Spiritual PracticeHuman Nature
Verse 6
ٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ يُرَآءُونَ
"Those who make show [of their deeds]"
Plain Understanding
Those who perform their worship only so that other people will see them, admire them, and praise them.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Riya" (showing off). This describes the hypocrites in Madinah (though the concept applies early on) who would pray beautifully when the Prophet ﷺ and believers were watching, but wouldn't pray at all when they were alone at home.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Riya is called the "Hidden Shirk" (Polytheism). When you pray to impress a human, you are treating that human as if they are the one who grants salvation. You have placed an idol in the very act designed to destroy idols.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Actions done for the sake of the creation will perish with the creation. Actions done for the Eternal will remain eternally."

Verse 7
وَيَمْنَعُونَ ٱلْمَاعُونَ
"And withhold [simple] assistance."
Plain Understanding
And on top of their corrupted prayers, they are so stingy they refuse to lend even the smallest, most basic household items to their neighbors.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Al-Ma'un" refers to things that people naturally borrow from one another and return—like a bucket, a needle, an axe, salt, or water. It costs the owner absolutely nothing to lend it.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ultimate spiritual contraction. They stand in prayer begging God for His massive, infinite Mercy, but their hearts are so tight and diseased that they refuse a drop of water or a needle to their neighbor. The Surah connects the cosmic (Judgment Day) with the microscopic (lending a bucket). If you fail the microscopic test of compassion, you will fail the cosmic test of Judgment.
