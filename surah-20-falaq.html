Surah Al-Falaq

The Daybreak • Makkah • 5 Verses
The Ultimate Shield. This Surah is a divine prescription against the terrifying external forces of the world: the creeping darkness, hidden magic, and the toxic gaze of envy. It teaches that the only safe refuge from the creation is the Creator.
Verse 1
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقِ
"Say, 'I seek refuge in the Lord of daybreak'"
Plain Understanding
Declare and pray: I flee for ultimate protection and safety to the Master and Creator of the breaking dawn.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"A'udhu" means to actively flee from something terrifying into a secure fortress. "Falaq" means to cleave or split open. God splits the dark night to bring forth the bright morning, and splits the seed to bring forth the plant. He is the Lord of new beginnings and sudden relief.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "night" is the darkness of ignorance, depression, and sin. Seeking refuge in the Lord of the Daybreak means asking Him to split the darkness of your ego and grant you the illuminating light of guidance.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not seek refuge in the creation, for it is as fragile as you." Realize that only the One who commands the cosmos to transition from pitch black to blinding light can truly protect your heart from despair.

Verse 2
مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
"From the evil of that which He created"
Plain Understanding
Protect me from all the harm, danger, and evil that exists within any of His creations.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
God created all things, and within the creation exists the capacity for harm (venomous animals, oppressive humans, diseases, accidents). This is a sweeping, general request for physical and spiritual protection from all external worldly harm.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Notice it says "the evil of what He created," affirming that evil is not an independent divine force. Evil is the absence of good, or the misuse of creation. The ultimate "evil of creation" that you must seek refuge from is your own Nafs (ego) when it disobeys.

Verse 3
وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ
"And from the evil of darkness when it settles"
Plain Understanding
And specifically, protect me from the hidden evils that emerge when the intensely dark night covers the earth.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Ghasiq" is the pitch-black night. Historically, the night was a time of immense vulnerability. When darkness settles (Waqab), thieves strike, wild predators hunt, and people are asleep and defenseless. It is a plea for safety when human vigilance is at its weakest.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "darkness that settles" represents periods of spiritual depression (Qabd) or moments when heedlessness overtakes the heart, allowing whispers of doubt and temptation to sneak in while the intellect is "asleep."

Verse 4
وَمِن شَرِّ ٱلنَّفَّـٰثَـٰتِ فِى ٱلْعُقَدِ
"And from the evil of the blowers in knots"
Plain Understanding
And from the evil of those who practice dark magic, sorcery, and witchcraft.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Refers to sorcerers who would tie knots in a string and blow upon them with incantations to cause harm. While originally revealed in Makkah, these Surahs were heavily recited later in Madinah when a man named Labeed bin Al-A'sam cast a dark spell on the Prophet ﷺ, causing him severe physical illness. Reciting these verses broke the spell entirely.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Metaphorically, the "blowers in knots" are the toxic people, cynics, or demonic whispers that try to untie your firm resolutions ("knots") of faith. They blow doubt onto your commitments to break your spiritual discipline.

Verse 5
وَمِن شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ
"And from the evil of an envier when he envies."
Plain Understanding
And protect me from the destructive malice of a jealous person when they actively project their envy upon me.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Hasad (envy) is desiring that a blessing be stripped away from someone else. It was the first sin committed in heaven (Iblis envying Adam) and on earth (Cain envying Abel). The "evil eye" (Ayn) is a very real, metaphysical consequence of intense envy that can cause physical and psychological harm.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The Prophet ﷺ said, "Envy consumes good deeds just as fire consumes wood." Seeking refuge from the envier also means begging God to cure your *own* heart from harboring envy toward others. If you envy, you become the villain of this verse.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Whoever envies the creation is implicitly objecting to the Creator's distribution of His grace." Envy is fundamentally a theological problem: it is an accusation that God made a mistake in giving someone else a blessing instead of you.
