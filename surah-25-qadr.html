Surah Al-Qadr

The Power / The Decree • Makkah • 5 Verses
The Night of Majesty. This Surah commemorates the single greatest night in the history of the cosmos: the descent of the Quran. It reveals a night of such concentrated spiritual gravity that a few hours of sincere devotion outweigh a lifetime of eighty-three years.
Verse 1
إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَـٰهُ فِى لَيْلَةِ ٱلْقَدْرِ
"Indeed, We sent the Qur'an down during the Night of Decree."
Plain Understanding
God declares with absolute authority that He sent down the Quran on a specific, immensely powerful night.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
According to Ibn Abbas, the entire Quran was brought down from the Preserved Tablet (Al-Lawh Al-Mahfuz) to the lowest heaven on this one night in Ramadan. From there, Jibreel brought it down to the Prophet ﷺ gradually over 23 years. "Qadr" means value, majesty, and destiny, because on this night the decrees for the upcoming year are assigned.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The descent of the Quran into the physical world mirrors the descent of Divine Light into the spiritual heart of the seeker. A heart that clears itself of the ego's noise and receives this light experiences its own personal, transformative "Night of Power."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Notice that God says "We sent *it* down." He hides the object (the Quran) in a pronoun out of sheer majesty and reverence, assuming the believer's heart is already intimately aware of the ultimate Gift being discussed.

Verse 2
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا لَيْلَةُ ٱلْقَدْرِ
"And what can make you know what is the Night of Decree?"
Plain Understanding
A profound rhetorical question emphasizing that the true magnitude of this night is completely beyond human imagination.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Whenever Allah uses the phrase "Wa ma adraka" (And what can make you know?) in the Quran, it serves as an attention-grabber, preparing the listener for a reality that defies human logic and calculation. It builds ultimate awe.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Human intellect is fundamentally incapable of measuring the true weight of Divine gifts. You cannot calculate spiritual grace with worldly mathematics; you can only stand in awe of it and submit.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The question intentionally forces the ego to admit its total ignorance. You only know what God chooses to reveal to you. The realization of your own intellectual poverty is the prerequisite for receiving His divine knowledge.

Verse 3
لَيْلَةُ ٱلْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍۢ
"The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months."
Plain Understanding
Worshipping on this single night is more heavily rewarded than worshipping continuously for one thousand months (over 83 years).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ was shown the lifespans of people from previous nations, some of whom worshipped God for hundreds of years. He felt deep sorrow, worrying his Ummah (whose average lifespan is 60-70 years) could never compete in good deeds. Out of pure mercy, God gave this night as an ultimate multiplier to compensate them.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Time is relative in the presence of the Divine. A single, solitary moment of absolute sincerity and presence with God is heavier in the eternal scales than an entire lifetime of heedless, mechanical worship. Quality of heart completely shatters the quantity of time.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He has given you a time that is short, so do not shorten it further by your heedlessness." This night is the ultimate spiritual "hack" for the bankrupt soul to catch up to the saints and spiritual elites of history.

Verse 4
تَنَزَّلُ ٱلْمَلَـٰٓئِكَةُ وَٱلرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم مِّن كُلِّ أَمْرٍۢ
"The angels and the Spirit descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter."
Plain Understanding
Countless angels, including Jibreel (The Spirit), come down to earth continuously on this night, bringing God's specific decrees for the upcoming year.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ said that on this night, the number of angels on earth is greater than the number of pebbles. They descend to say "Ameen" to the prayers of the believers, to greet them, and to execute the Qadr (destiny)—who will live, die, receive provision, or face trials in the coming 365 days.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The descent of the angels brings a heavy, thick atmosphere of Sakinah (tranquility) to the earth. A heart that has fasted properly and emptied itself of worldly noise can literally feel the spiritual gravity and dense peace of their presence in the late hours of the night.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The decrees for your entire year are being finalized tonight. Stand in absolute humility and spiritual poverty, asking the King to write your name among the forgiven, the protected, and the beloved before the pen lifts.

Verse 5
سَلَـٰمٌ هِىَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ ٱلْفَجْرِ
"Peace it is until the emergence of dawn."
Plain Understanding
The entire night is a state of pure peace, safety, and tranquility, completely unbroken until the sun comes up.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Unlike other nights where demons roam and whisper, the devils are chained and stripped of their power on this specific night. It is a night of pure salvation where no evil can be decreed, only peace, mercy, and liberation from the Hellfire.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ultimate goal of the spiritual path is "Salam" (Peace)—a heart totally surrendered to God, free from anxiety about the past or fear of the future. The night ends at dawn, just as the darkness of the Dunya ultimately ends at the bright dawn of the Akhirah.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Peace is not the absence of conflict in the physical world; it is the absolute presence of God in the heart. Whoever secures this state of peace on Laylat al-Qadr secures their eternity.
