Surah Al-Buruj

The Constellations • Makkah • 22 Verses
The Anatomy of True Victory. A haunting and majestic Surah recounting the believers who were burned alive in trenches. It redefines success: the tyrant king who burned them failed, and the martyrs who burned won the "Great Victory" by protecting their faith.
Verse 1
وَٱلسَّمَآءِ ذَاتِ ٱلْبُرُوجِ
"By the sky containing great stars,"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the sky and its massive constellations, star clusters, and cosmic fortresses.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Al-Buruj" refers to massive structures, forts, or the 12 mansions of the stars (Zodiac). The Arabs saw these starry formations as towering, impenetrable fortresses in the sky. Allah swears by the ultimate, unconquerable fortresses above to prepare us for a story about a tyrant below who thought his worldly fortress made him a god.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Just as the physical sky has "Buruj" (fortresses) to protect it from ascending devils, the spiritual heart must have fortresses of Dhikr (remembrance) and Taqwa (piety) to protect it from the whisperings of the ego.

Verse 2
وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْمَوْعُودِ
"And [by] the promised Day,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the Day of Judgment, which has been unconditionally promised to all of humanity.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Justice in the Dunya is often delayed or denied, which can cause the heart to despair. But the "Promised Day" is the anchor of the believer. God’s promise is the ultimate guarantee that no tear falls unrecorded and no drop of blood is spilled without an accounting.

Verse 3
وَشَاهِدٍۢ وَمَشْهُودٍۢ
"And [by] the witness and what is witnessed,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by everything that bears witness, and everything that is being observed.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
In the context of the upcoming story, the "witness" is the tyrant kings and the crowds watching the torture, and the "witnessed" are the believers burning in the fire. In a broader sense, the Prophet ﷺ is a witness over his Ummah, and the Day of Judgment is the ultimate witnessed event.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The highest station of faith is *Mushahadah* (witnessing). When the heart becomes a pure "witness" to the reality of God, the terror of the physical world (like being thrown into a fire) becomes entirely secondary to the beauty of the Divine Presence.

Verse 4
قُتِلَ أَصْحَـٰبُ ٱلْأُخْدُودِ
"Destroyed [i.e., cursed] were the companions of the trench"
Plain Understanding
May the evil men who dug the massive trench and threw the believers into it be entirely cursed and destroyed.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This refers to a historical event in Najran (Yemen) led by a Jewish tyrant king named Dhu Nuwas (pre-Islam). A young believing boy had successfully called the entire city to believe in the One True God. Furious, the king ordered massive trenches (Ukhdud) to be dug, filled them with raging fire, and gave the believers a choice: renounce your faith or burn. They chose the fire.

Verse 5
ٱلنَّارِ ذَاتِ ٱلْوَقُودِ
"[Containing] the fire full of fuel,"
Plain Understanding
A trench filled with an intensely blazing fire, continuously fed with heavy fuel to keep it roaring.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The fire of the tyrant was fueled by wood. But the fire of Hell (which the tyrant will face) is fueled by the tyrants themselves. The pain they inflicted in the Dunya is merely a shadow of the justice awaiting them.

"When they were sitting near it"

While the king and his elite guards sat comfortably in chairs around the edge of the burning trench.

Observe the chilling apathy of the hardened ego. They did not just kill; they sat comfortably and made a spectacle of human suffering. When the heart loses the light of God, it is capable of a cruelty that shames wild beasts.

Verse 7
وَهُمْ عَلَىٰ مَا يَفْعَلُونَ بِٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ شُهُودٌۭ
"And they, to what they were doing to the believers, were witnesses."
Plain Understanding
They actively watched, witnessed, and enjoyed the horrific torture they were inflicting upon the faithful believers.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Among the victims was a woman carrying an infant. She hesitated at the edge of the fire out of love for her baby. Miraculously, the infant spoke to her: "O mother, be patient, for you are upon the Truth!" Upon hearing this, she plunged into the fire with her child. The king watched this entirely unmoved.

Verse 8

"And they resented them not except because they believed in Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Praiseworthy,"
Plain Understanding
The believers committed no crime, treason, or theft. The only reason the king hated them and burned them was their pure faith in the Mighty, Praiseworthy God.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
When you carry the light of Tawheed (Monotheism), the darkness will naturally resent you. Do not expect applause from a society deeply invested in falsehood. The resentment of the ignorant is the badge of honor for the believer.

Verse 9
ٱلَّذِى لَهُۥ مُلْكُ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ شَهِيدٌ
"To whom belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth. And Allah, over all things, is Witness."
Plain Understanding
This God they believed in is the absolute Owner of the entire universe. And God was watching and witnessing everything the tyrants were doing.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The tyrant sat as a "witness" (v. 7) to his own cruelty, feeling powerful. But he forgot that the True King of the Heavens was a "Witness" (Shaheed) over him. The watcher was being watched. Human power is an illusion acting within the theater of Divine Sovereignty.

Verse 10
إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ فَتَنُوا۟ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنَـٰتِ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَتُوبُوا۟ فَلَهُمْ عَذَابُ جَهَنَّمَ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابُ ٱلْحَرِيقِ
"Indeed, those who have tortured the believing men and believing women and then have not repented will have the punishment of Hell, and they will have the punishment of the Burning Fire."
Plain Understanding
Those who burned and persecuted the believers, and refused to repent for it, will face the eternal fires of Hell.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Look at the sheer, unimaginable mercy of God. Hasan Al-Basri commented on this verse: "Look at His generosity! They killed His beloved friends, they burned His saints alive, and yet He *still* opens the door and offers them repentance ('and then have not repented'). If they had asked for forgiveness, He would have forgiven them!"

Verse 11
إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّـٰلِحَـٰتِ لَهُمْ جَنَّـٰتٌ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَـٰرُ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْكَبِيرُ
"Indeed, those who have believed and done righteous deeds will have gardens beneath which rivers flow. That is the great attainment."
Plain Understanding
As for the believers who were thrown into the fire, they will be given eternal gardens with flowing rivers. God declares *that* to be the ultimate, massive victory.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This verse redefines victory. The believers were burned to ash; their bodies were destroyed. In worldly metrics, they lost entirely. But God calls their death "Al-Fawz Al-Kabir" (The Great Victory). Why? Because the goal of life is not to preserve your biology; the goal is to preserve your theology. They died, but their faith remained unburned. That is victory.

Verse 12
إِنَّ بَطْشَ رَبِّكَ لَشَدِيدٌ
"Indeed, the vengeance of your Lord is severe."
Plain Understanding
Truly, the grip and punishment of your Lord is overwhelmingly intense.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The tyrant King thought his "grip" on the believers was severe because he threw them in a ditch. God reminds the tyrants of Makkah that human torture is temporary and frail. When the Divine Grip closes on a tyrant, there is no escape and no end to the severity.

Verse 13
إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ يُبْدِئُ وَيُعِيدُ
"Indeed, it is He who originates [creation] and repeats."
Plain Understanding
God is the One who starts creation from nothing, and He is the One who will bring it back to life after death.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The tyrant thought that by burning the believers to ash, he was ending their existence forever. Allah promises: I originated them the first time, and I will seamlessly repeat their creation to reward them, and repeat yours to punish you.

Verse 14
وَهُوَ ٱلْغَفُورُ ٱلْوَدُودُ
"And He is the Forgiving, the Affectionate,"
Plain Understanding
And alongside His severe justice, He is intensely forgiving and deeply, intimately loving.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Al-Wadud" is not just general love (Hubb); it is an active, affectionate, expressive love. To pair "Severe in Vengeance" (v. 12) directly with "The Affectionate" is a profound theological balance. He destroys the arrogant tyrant out of justice, and He embraces the broken, repentant sinner out of pure, affectionate love.

Verse 15
ذُو ٱلْعَرْشِ ٱلْمَجِيدُ
"Honorable Owner of the Throne,"
Plain Understanding
He is the possessor of the massive, majestic Throne.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The tyrant sat on his little wooden throne watching the fire (v. 6). God contrasts that pathetic display with His own Throne, which encompasses the heavens and the earth. Do not fear a king who sits on wood when you are protected by the Owner of the Cosmic Throne.

Verse 16
فَعَّالٌۭ لِّمَا يُرِيدُ
"Effecter of what He intends."
Plain Understanding
He does absolutely whatever He wants to do, without exception or hindrance.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
If God is all-powerful, why did He let the believers burn? Because He is "Fa'aalun lima yureed" (He does what He intends). His wisdom requires tests. He intended for those believers to skip the trials of old age and sickness and transition directly into the highest ranks of martyrdom. His Will operates on a canvas too large for human anxiety to comprehend.

Have you heard the historical accounts of the massive, armed forces of the past?
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
A rhetorical question to awaken the intellect. God draws our attention away from the current threats in Makkah to the graveyard of history. Look at the armies that thought they were invincible. Where are they now?

Verse 18
فِرْعَوْنَ وَثَمُودَ
"[Those of] Pharaoh and Thamud?"
Plain Understanding
Specifically, the seemingly untouchable armies of Pharaoh in Egypt and the rock-carving tribe of Thamud?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Pharaoh had military supremacy. Thamud had architectural supremacy. Both weaponized their strength against the Prophets. Both were wiped off the face of the earth. God reminds the Quraish: you are weaker than them, so do not test the Grip of the Lord.

"While Allah encompasses them from behind."
Plain Understanding
They think they are getting away with their denial, but God completely surrounds them from all sides, blocking any escape.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Muhit" means to completely encircle. Just as the physical sky (v. 1) encircles the earth, God's power entirely encircles the tyrants. They are operating inside His prison, completely unaware that the doors are already locked.

"But this is an honored Qur'an"

Allah uses the same word "Majeed" (Glorious/Honorable) to describe His Throne (v. 15) and His Book (v. 21). To reject the Quran is to reject the direct manifestation of the Glory of the Throne on earth.

"[Inscribed] in a Preserved Slate."
Plain Understanding
This Quran is permanently recorded and fiercely protected in the highest heavens on the Preserved Tablet (Al-Lawh Al-Mahfuz).
