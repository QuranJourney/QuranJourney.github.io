Mushaf: #106 | Chronological: #29

Surah Quraysh

The Tribe of Quraysh • Makkah • 4 Verses
The Debt of Privilege. A targeted reminder to the elite of Makkah that their economic monopolies and physical safety are not the result of their own genius, but are exclusive gifts from the Lord of the Kaaba, demanding their absolute worship in return.
Verse 1
لِإِيلَـٰفِ قُرَيْشٍ
"For the accustomed security of the Quraysh -"
Plain Understanding
Due to the deep familiarity, unity, and secure habits granted to the tribe of Quraysh.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Ilaf" means bringing together, creating a pact, or establishing a secure habit. The Quraysh were respected across Arabia as the "People of God" simply because they lived around the Kaaba. This association gave them a divine diplomatic immunity in a land otherwise plagued by tribal warfare.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Security and familiarity can breed spiritual heedlessness. When a massive blessing (like waking up safe every day) becomes a daily habit (Ilaf), the ego stops seeing it as a miraculous gift and begins to assume it is an inherent entitlement.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Whoever does not thank Him for His graces exposes them to cessation." Do not let the familiarity of a blessing blind you to the Majesty of the Blesser. The moment you take safety for granted is the moment you risk losing it.

Verse 2
إِۦلَـٰفِهِمْ رِحْلَةَ ٱلشِّتَآءِ وَٱلصَّيْفِ
"Their accustomed security [in] the caravan of winter and summer -"
Plain Understanding
Specifically, their established, safe habit of sending huge trade caravans to Yemen in the winter and to Syria in the summer.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
In the violent, lawless deserts of ancient Arabia, tribes constantly raided and robbed each other's merchants. But no one dared attack the caravans of Quraysh out of reverence for the Kaaba. Because of this uninterrupted safety, the Quraysh built a massive, wealthy economic empire.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The external caravans travel continuously to gather wealth, but the internal caravan of the soul must travel continuously toward the Divine. Do not exhaust your life organizing the winter and summer trades of the Dunya while entirely neglecting the eternal journey of the Akhirah.

Verse 3
فَلْيَعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّ هَـٰذَا ٱلْبَيْتِ
"Let them worship the Lord of this House,"
Plain Understanding
Therefore, in deep gratitude, they must exclusively worship the Master and Owner of this sacred House (the Kaaba).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This is the logical conclusion. God doesn't ask them to pay a tax; He asks for their loyalty. Since the Kaaba is the entire source of their political and economic supremacy, it is deeply hypocritical to worship the 360 lifeless stone idols sitting inside the House, instead of worshipping the actual Lord of the House.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ego tends to worship the "House" (the system, the business, the physical structure, the status). The purified soul worships the "Lord of the House." Never confuse the physical vessel of the blessing with the unseen Source of the blessing.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He facilitated your worldly affairs so you would have the time and peace of mind to worship Him, not so you would worship the affairs themselves." Prosperity is meant to free your hands for prayer, not to tie them to the market.

Verse 4
ٱلَّذِىٓ أَطْعَمَهُم مِّن جُوعٍۢ وَءَامَنَهُم مِّنْ خَوْفٍۭ
"Who has fed them, [saving them] from hunger and made them safe, [saving them] from fear."
Plain Understanding
The Lord who saved them from starvation by providing abundant food, and saved them from terror by providing total, impenetrable security.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Makkah is a barren, rocky, volcanic valley where literally no crops can grow. By all geographic logic, its people should have starved. Yet, due to Abraham's ancient prayer and God's decree, the finest fruits and goods from all over the world are imported directly to their doorsteps. Furthermore, He protected them from the terrifying Army of the Elephant (Surah 105).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Hunger and Fear are the two great paralyses of the human condition. When God removes them from your life, the only appropriate response is absolute, loving submission. To use the physical energy of His food and the psychological peace of His safety to rebel against Him is the ultimate form of treachery.
