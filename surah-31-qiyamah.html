Surah Al-Qiyamah

The Resurrection • Makkah • 40 Verses
The Awakening of the Conscience. An intense psychological dive into the human soul, the agonizing physical process of death, and the undeniable reality of Resurrection, culminating in the ultimate reward: gazing upon the Creator.
Verse 1
لَآ أُقْسِمُ بِيَوْمِ ٱلْقِيَـٰمَةِ
"I swear by the Day of Resurrection"
Plain Understanding
God swears an absolute oath by the massive, inevitable Day of Judgment.
Historical Context
The "La" (No) before the oath is an Arabic linguistic tool meaning: "No, the matter is not as you pagans claim it to be! I swear by the Resurrection..." It actively strikes down their denial before the oath even begins.

Verse 2
وَلَآ أُقْسِمُ بِٱلنَّفْسِ ٱللَّوَّامَةِ
"And I swear by the reproaching soul."
Plain Understanding
And God swears by the human conscience—the inner voice that constantly blames and criticizes you when you do wrong.
Purification of the Self
The Nafs al-Lawwamah (Self-Reproaching Soul) is the second stage of spiritual growth. It is better than the Nafs al-Ammarah (the soul that blindly commands to evil) but not yet the Nafs al-Mutma'innah (the soul at peace). Guilt is a divine gift; it means your spiritual heart is still alive and fighting the ego.
Divine Wisdom
Why swear by the Day of Judgment *and* the conscience? Because the conscience is the internal, psychological Day of Judgment happening right now in your chest. If you listen to the internal judgment today, you will be safe from the external Judgment tomorrow.

Verse 3
أَيَحْسَبُ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ أَلَّن نَّجْمَعَ عِظَامَهُۥ
"Does man think that We will not assemble his bones?"
Plain Understanding
Does the arrogant human being really believe God is incapable of putting his decayed skeleton back together?
Historical Context
A pagan named 'Ali ibn Rabi'ah brought an old, crumbling bone to the Prophet ﷺ, crushed it into dust, blew it into the wind, and mocked, "Do you really think your God can rebuild this?" This verse is the direct, cosmic answer.

Verse 4
بَلَىٰ قَـٰدِرِينَ عَلَىٰٓ أَن نُّسَوِّىَ بَنَانَهُۥ
"Yes. [We are] Able [even] to proportion his fingertips."
Plain Understanding
Absolutely! God is not only able to assemble large bones, but He is able to perfectly reconstruct the intricate, microscopic details of your very fingertips.
Divine Wisdom
Fingertips (fingerprints) are the ultimate symbol of unique individual identity. God is saying: I will not just resurrect a generic human body; I will restore your exact, unique, undeniable identity down to the microscopic ridges on your fingers to hold you personally accountable.

Verse 5
بَلْ يُرِيدُ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ لِيَفْجُرَ أَمَامَهُۥ
"But man desires to continue in sin."
Plain Understanding
The truth is, man doesn't deny resurrection because of lack of evidence; he denies it because he wants a free pass to keep sinning in the future.
Purification of the Self
A devastating psychological diagnosis. Theological doubt is often just a smokescreen for moral rebellion. If there is no Judgment, there are no rules. The ego kills the concept of tomorrow (Akhirah) so it can gorge itself on today (Dunya).

Plain Understanding
He mockingly asks, "So, when exactly is this supposed Day of Judgment going to happen?"
Historical Context
This is a question of sarcasm, not inquiry. They asked for a calendar date to prove it was fake, rather than preparing their souls for its inevitable arrival.

Since the ultimate destination is inevitably God, the spiritual master runs *to* God in the Dunya, so that on Judgment Day, he is not running *from* Him in terror, but arriving at the home of his Beloved.

Every person will be given a detailed report of the deeds they did early in life, the deeds they delayed, and the legacy they left behind.
Divine Wisdom
You are not just judged for your actions; you are judged for your omissions ("kept back"). The good you intended but delayed out of laziness, and the evil traditions you started that continued after your death, will all be laid bare.

Plain Understanding
God interrupts the narrative to comfort the Prophet: Do not urgently move your tongue to repeat the Quran while Jibreel is reciting it out of fear you will forget it.
Historical Context
Whenever the Angel Jibreel brought heavy revelation, the Prophet ﷺ would rapidly repeat the words simultaneously, terrified he might forget a single syllable of God's Word. God gently commands him to relax and just listen.

Purification of the Self
The repetition intensifies the terror. The first "Woe" is for the destruction he will face at the moment of death (v. 26-30). The second "Woe" is for the eternal terror he will face on the Day of Resurrection. His swagger is sandwiched between two total annihilations.
