Mushaf: #104 | Chronological: #32

Surah Al-Humazah

The Traducer • Makkah • 9 Verses
The Anatomy of Arrogance. A ruthless takedown of the corporate, wealth-driven ego that mocks and degrades others. It reveals the exact, terrifying form of divine justice awaiting the one who destroys reputations: a fire designed specifically to crush the arrogant heart.
Verse 1
وَيْلٌۭ لِّكُلِّ هُمَزَةٍۢ لُّمَزَةٍ
"Woe to every scorner and mocker"
Plain Understanding
Absolute destruction and a severe curse belong to every person who slanders, backbites, and mocks others.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Revealed regarding specific Makkan elites (like Umayyah bin Khalaf or Al-Akhnas bin Shariq) who used to publicly and privately insult the Prophet ﷺ and the poor Muslims. They weaponized their wealth to humiliate the vulnerable.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Humazah" is mocking someone with words (gossip, slander). "Lumazah" is mocking them with gestures (rolling the eyes, pointing, sarcastic expressions). The ego elevates itself by stepping on the dignity of others. True nobility is found in elevating others, not destroying them.

Verse 2
ٱلَّذِى جَمَعَ مَالًۭا وَعَدَّدَهُۥ
"Who collects wealth and [continuously] counts it."
Plain Understanding
The one who obsessively hoards money and constantly, anxiously counts it out of greed.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The root of their arrogant mocking (v. 1) is their wealth (v. 2). They believe their net worth makes them a superior class of human. Counting the wealth repeatedly is a symptom of extreme anxiety, proving that the Dunya brings absolutely no peace to the heart that worships it.

Verse 3
يَحْسَبُ أَنَّ مَالَهُۥٓ أَخْلَدَهُۥ
"He thinks that his wealth will make him immortal."
Plain Understanding
He lives under the complete delusion that his massive fortune will make him live forever and protect him from death.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
They built massive fortresses, hired guards, and lived in such luxury that they acted as if they could bribe the Angel of Death. Wealth provides a dangerous psychological illusion of invulnerability.

Verse 4
كَلَّا ۖ لَيُنۢبَذَنَّ فِى ٱلْحُطَمَةِ
"No! He will surely be thrown into the Crusher."
Plain Understanding
Absolutely not! He will forcefully be tossed like trash into the crushing, shattering fire of Hell.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Yunbathanna" means to throw away something worthless and despised. He spent his life viewing the poor as trash, so God treats him as trash. "Al-Hutamah" is the fire that violently crushes and breaks bones, perfectly mirroring how he crushed people's reputations with his tongue.
PunishmentDivine JusticeHell
Verse 5
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا ٱلْحُطَمَةُ
"And what can make you know what is the Crusher?"
Plain Understanding
And what could possibly make you comprehend the sheer, indescribable terror of this crushing Fire?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
A recurring Quranic mechanism to shatter human presumption. You cannot imagine it, calculate it, or buy your way out of it with your counted wealth.
WarningThe Unseen
Verse 6
نَارُ ٱللَّهِ ٱلْمُوقَدَةُ
"It is the fire of Allah, [eternally] kindled,"
Plain Understanding
It is the raging, blazing fire lit and sustained by God Himself.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This is the only place in the entire Quran where Hell is explicitly called "the Fire of *Allah*." By personally attaching His own name to it, He indicates that this fire is fueled by His personal, divine wrath against those who violate the dignity of His creation.
HellAttributes of Allah
Verse 7
ٱلَّتِى تَطَّلِعُ عَلَى ٱلْأَفْـِٔدَةِ
"Which mounts directed at the hearts."
Plain Understanding
A fire so intensely piercing that it burns straight through the body and engulfs the physical heart while the person is still alive.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Why does the fire specifically target the heart (Af'idah)? Because the heart was the source of his arrogance, his obsessive love for wealth, and his malicious hatred for the believers. The punishment directly targets the origin of the disease. It also burns the heart because that is exactly where he felt the dark, twisted joy of mocking others.

Verse 8
إِنَّهَا عَلَيْهِم مُّؤْصَدَةٌۭ
"Indeed, Hellfire will be closed down upon them"
Plain Understanding
It will be tightly sealed and permanently locked over them like a vault, leaving absolutely no means of escape.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
They locked their wealth away in heavy safes and vaults, hoarding it from the poor. In an act of pure, terrifying poetic justice, God locks them inside the vault of Hellfire.
HellPunishment
Verse 9
فِى عَمَدٍۢ مُّمَدَّدَةٍۭ
"In extended columns."
Plain Understanding
They will be locked inside massive, towering columns of fire stretching endlessly.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
In the Dunya, they leaned on the tall, impressive pillars of their worldly corporations, palaces, and status to intimidate others. Now they are chained to the towering pillars of Hell. The architecture of their pride becomes the architecture of their eternal torment.
