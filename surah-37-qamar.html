Surah Al-Qamar

The Moon • Makkah • 55 Verses
The Cosmic Warning. Opening with the miraculous splitting of the moon, this Surah delivers a fast-paced, devastating review of ancient civilizations that denied their Prophets, repeating a powerful, rhythmic challenge: "Is there anyone who will remember?"
Verse 1
ٱقْتَرَبَتِ ٱلسَّاعَةُ وَٱنشَقَّ ٱلْقَمَرُ
"The Hour has come near, and the moon has split [in two]."
Plain Understanding
The Day of Judgment is rapidly approaching, evidenced by the literal splitting of the moon in the sky.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The pagan leaders demanded a physical miracle, pointing to the sky: "Split the moon if you are a Prophet." The Prophet ﷺ prayed, and God split the moon into two distinct halves, with Mount Hira visible between them. It was a staggering, undeniable historical event witnessed by caravans outside the city as well.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The splitting of the moon is a macrocosmic sign of a microcosmic reality: when the Light of the Divine Command truly hits the heart, the hard, stony "moon" of the ego is completely cracked open, making way for the approach of spiritual awakening (The Hour).

"And if they see a miracle, they turn away and say, 'Passing magic.'"
Plain Understanding
Even when confronted with an undeniable, massive miracle, they actively ignore it, claiming it is just a continuous, powerful illusion.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"If the eye of the heart is blind, a thousand suns will not give it sight." They demanded a miracle, but when God provided it, their arrogance immediately manufactured an excuse. Lack of faith is rarely a lack of evidence; it is a rebellion of the will.
